Moving Forward

I began working at sixteen and haven't stopped. Once I graduated High School in 2011, I put in a lot of work at the Garden of the Gods Trading Post; however, I realized that working there wasn't going to send my life on the right direction. I decided to join the United States Marine Corps. After fulfilling my obligated service time of four years, I took full advanatage of the educational benefits that I earned. As a result, I am now a senior at University of California, Riverside. I look forward to pursuing a single subject credential program and a masters in history.

I am more than happy to assist transitioning veterans from active duty to the civilian sector because it can be quite overwhelming. There a limitless educational benefits that service members receive, but that the and federal government are willing to provide for veterans. The problem is a lot of veterans are unaware of these programs or that the process to access them can be frustrating. I hope to alleviate those conflicts and make applications as smooth as possible.
